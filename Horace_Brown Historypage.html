History of Physics

Physics, one of the oldest academic disciplines, has its roots in ancient times with the study of natural phenomena such as motion and astronomy. Early thinkers like Aristotle and Archimedes laid foundational ideas that influenced physics for centuries.

In the 17th century, the scientific revolution brought about transformative changes with the work of scientists such as Galileo Galilei, Isaac Newton, and Johannes Kepler. Their discoveries in mechanics and celestial motion shaped classical physics.

Over time, new branches emerged, such as electromagnetism, thermodynamics, and quantum mechanics, with key contributions from scientists like James Clerk Maxwell, Albert Einstein, and Niels Bohr. Today, physics continues to drive advancements in technology and our understanding of the universe.
